What is Global Warming?

Global warming refers to the long-term heating of Earth's climate system observed since the pre-industrial period, primarily due to human activities — especially the burning of fossil fuels — which raises heat-trapping greenhouse gas levels in the atmosphere.

The effects are no longer a distant warning. Heatwaves are more intense, wildfires burn longer, hurricanes carry more rain, glaciers are retreating, and coastal communities face existential threat from rising seas. Ecosystems that took millennia to evolve are collapsing in a span of decades.

What's Driving It?

Global warming is driven primarily by human-caused emissions of greenhouse gases. Here are the largest contributors:

⛽

Fossil Fuels

Burning coal, oil, and gas for electricity, transport, and industry releases enormous amounts of CO₂ — the single largest driver of warming.

🌳

Deforestation

Forests absorb CO₂. When they're cleared for agriculture or development, that stored carbon is released and future absorption is lost.

🐄

Agriculture

Livestock produce methane, a greenhouse gas over 25 times more potent than CO₂. Fertilizers release nitrous oxide, another powerful warmer.

🏭

Industry

Cement, steel, and chemical production are carbon-intensive. Industrial processes account for roughly a quarter of global emissions.

What You Can Do

Individual choices matter — but systemic change matters more. Here's where you can make a difference:

🚲 Rethink Transport

Walk, bike, use public transit, or switch to an electric vehicle when possible.

🥗 Eat Less Meat

Reducing meat consumption — especially beef — significantly lowers your carbon footprint.

⚡ Save Energy

Better insulation, efficient appliances, and renewable electricity tariffs add up.

🗳️ Vote & Advocate

Support climate-conscious leaders and policies. Collective action drives systemic change.
