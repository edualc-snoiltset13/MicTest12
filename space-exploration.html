Mission Timeline

From Sputnik
to the Webb Telescope

Seven decades of missions that redefined what it means to be human — and what it means to be small.

1957
Sputnik 1 — The First Satellite

A 83 kg aluminium sphere launched by the Soviet Union on 4 October 1957. Its radio beeps, audible by amateur operators worldwide, announced the dawn of the Space Age.

🇷🇺 USSR
1961
Vostok 1 — First Human in Space

Yuri Gagarin completed one orbit of Earth in 108 minutes. "The Earth is blue," he reported. It remains one of the most significant moments in human history.

🇷🇺 USSR
1969
Apollo 11 — Moon Landing

Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to set foot on another world. An estimated 530 million people watched on television. "One giant leap for mankind."

🇺🇸 NASA
1990
Hubble Space Telescope

Deployed into low Earth orbit, Hubble fundamentally changed our understanding of the universe — measuring the age of the cosmos, discovering dark energy, and photographing distant galaxies.

🇺🇸 NASA / ESA
2004
Mars Rovers — Spirit & Opportunity

Designed for 90-day missions, Opportunity survived for 15 years. Together they confirmed that liquid water once flowed on Mars, fundamentally changing the search for extraterrestrial life.

🇺🇸 NASA / JPL
2015
New Horizons — Pluto Flyby

After a 9-year, 5-billion km journey, New Horizons revealed Pluto as a complex world with mountains of ice and a vast nitrogen heart. Every pixel of those images took 4.5 hours to arrive at Earth.

🇺🇸 NASA
1971
Salyut 1 — First Space Station

The Soviet Union launched the first crewed space station. It proved humans could live and work in orbit for extended periods — the precursor to everything that followed.

🇷🇺 USSR
1998
ISS — Continuous Human Presence

Construction of the International Space Station began, involving 15 nations. Since November 2000, humans have lived aboard continuously — the longest unbroken human presence off Earth.

🌍 15 Nations
2012
Curiosity — Roving on Mars

A car-sized nuclear-powered rover, Curiosity has driven over 30 km across Gale Crater. It confirmed Mars was once habitable — with the right chemistry and liquid water for microbial life.

🇺🇸 NASA / JPL
2020
SpaceX Crew Dragon

The first commercially operated crewed spacecraft to reach the ISS marked the beginning of the commercial spaceflight era — making access to orbit a competitive market for the first time.

🚀 SpaceX
2021
James Webb Space Telescope

JWST's first deep-field image contained thousands of galaxies in a patch of sky the size of a grain of sand held at arm's length — including light from just 600 million years after the Big Bang.

🌍 NASA / ESA / CSA
2024 →
Artemis — Return to the Moon

NASA's Artemis programme aims to return humans to the lunar surface for the first time since 1972 — including the first woman and first person of colour to walk on the Moon. Mars follows.

🇺🇸 NASA
