GenevaAI Policy

47 nations back landmark AI safety treaty in surprise overnight breakthrough

By Lena Mwangi · Filed 06:14 GMT

Negotiators in Geneva agreed to a binding framework that requires independent auditing of frontier AI models above a defined compute threshold, with mandatory disclosure of catastrophic-risk evaluations. The accord, two years in the making, will now go to a ratification vote on Tuesday.

The treaty draws heavily from the joint U.S.-EU-China working group recommendations published last September. Notably absent from the signatories list are India and Brazil, both of which requested an additional 30-day review.

“This is the first time the international community has agreed that the most powerful AI systems require the same level of oversight as nuclear materials.” — UN Secretary-General
