Formula 1

The Pinnacle of Motorsport

What is Formula 1?

Formula 1 (F1) is the highest class of international single-seater racing, governed by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA). Each season, the world's best drivers and teams compete across a global calendar of Grands Prix, pushing the limits of engineering, strategy, and human skill at speeds exceeding 350 km/h.

How It Works

Practice

Teams fine-tune their cars across free practice sessions before qualifying.

Qualifying

A knockout format (Q1, Q2, Q3) sets the starting grid for race day.

The Race

Drivers battle over a set distance, managing tyres, fuel, and pit stops.

Two World Championships

  • Drivers' Championship — awarded to the driver with the most points.
  • Constructors' Championship — awarded to the most successful team.
